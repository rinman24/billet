01

The mark

A berth rack: two columns, three rows. Three berths are posted (accent-filled), three are left open. Square-first, so it drops straight into a favicon or app icon. Never fill all six — billet always leaves room.

Grid2 × 3 berthsPosted3 of 6 · magenta, mint, magenta
Berth ratio1 : 0.7Clearspace1 berth on every side
Corner radius0.15 × berth widthMinimum size16px

Open berths are outline-only in ink; below ~32px the outline is replaced by a solid recessive fill #9C8BB2 so berths stay crisp down to 16px.

03

Typography

Two families. One does words, one does machines. Space Grotesk for wordmark, display, headings, body, and UI; JetBrains Mono for code, CLI, labels, status, and data. The wordmark is always lowercase, Space Grotesk 600, tracking −0.045em, and stays ink — never colored.

04

Voice

Terse and lowercase. No exclamation. Present tense — posting, not will post. Status is a color, then a word. Commands read like a sentence.

# the command surface
billet post <repo>   post a repo's devcontainer to the Host
billet ls            list posted workspaces
billet shell <repo>  open a shell in a workspace
billet logs <repo>   tail workspace logs
billet rm <repo>     free the berth
05

Do / Don't

Do

  • Keep the wordmark ink — never colored.
  • 3 posted · 3 open — always leave berths open.
  • Keep the mark upright and flat.
  • Magenta = running; mint = building.

Don't

  • Color the wordmark.
  • Fill every berth.
  • Rotate or skew the rack.
  • Add glow, gradient, or shadow to the mark.
  • Stretch, condense, or crowd the wordmark.
